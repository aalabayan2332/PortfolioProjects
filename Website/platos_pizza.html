Plato's Pizza

This project was my first attempt to join the Maven's Challenge which is a very popular data challenge in LinkedIn. I mainly used Excel for this project and only used Pivot Tables as its backend. During the time that I was crafting this, I came across Other Level's YouTube Channel which is very popular because of their videos teaching how to create amazing dynamic dashboards using Excel only. Yes. No Macro, PowerQuery, or PowerPivot etc. Just Pivot Tables and visualizations. I also wanted to know how sustainable is this going to be in the long run which I found the hard way at work (spoiler alert: it's not!).

Whenever I create dashboard, I always think of it as if the user will be reading a book: read from the left, finish reading at the bottom right. This means that if you want to capture your reader's attention right away, it should be compelling right from the very start. In the context of building a dashboard, the most important details should be at the top-left part which are the summary statistics. I took the deductive approach and further broke the figures down as you look from top to bottom. The 2nd line looks at a more granular view of the sales performance. We start looking at product performance. For the conclusion, I had put it in the bottom-right so the reader would be "forced" to read the whole thing. 😅

I wanted to put it here to show that Excel could be as powerful as any data visualization tools if you push the boundaries of it. In my opinion, in terms of customization, Excel is a better visualization tool compared to Tableau and Power BI. If you're working with a small dataset, working with Excel could be a good option.
